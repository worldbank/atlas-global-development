Vulnerability and adaptation to climate-related hazards

Extreme weather events, such as floods, droughts, tornadoes, and heatwaves occur more often and are stronger due to climate change and impact more than half the world’s population. A third of those affected are also highly vulnerable and require adaptation to a changing climate.

Global mean temperature

Several indicators provide evidence of a changing climate. Globally, temperatures have been steadily increasing compared to the average for the period of 1850-1900.

Precipitation

The impacts of a changing climate not only affects average and extreme temperatures. Changing precipitation patterns are also affected, and irregular precipitation, droughts and floods are impacting agricultural production possibilities as well as the health and safety of individuals.
