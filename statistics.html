Intro

We are missing a lot of data.

Note: More than 772 million children (57% of children age 5-14) are missing a comparable learning assessment. 292 million children are in a country with no recent data about stunting (22% of children under 5). 209 million children’s births are not registered in their countries CRVS (16% of children under 5).

For learning assessments, the % missing is greater than 50% of children in East Asia, South Asia, and Sub-Saharan Africa. More than 50% of children in East Asia & Pacific and MENA are missing stunting data. Around half of all children under age 5 in East Asia & Pacific & Sub-Saharan Africa did not have their birth registered.

For learning assessments, the number shown is the count of all children age 5-14 living in a country where no internationally comparable learning assessment has taken place since 2019. For Stunting, the number shown is the count of all children under 5 living in a country where no stunting data has been collected since 2019. For birth registration, the number shown is the count of all children under 5 living in a country multiplied times the proportion of births not registered in the country using the CRVS. To produce the proportion of births not registered, the CRVS is compared to other data sources, such as the DHS or census.
