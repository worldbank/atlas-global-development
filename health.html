SDG 3 goal: Ensure healthy lives and well-being for all ages​

Target 3.8: Achieve universal health coverage, including financial

risk protection, access to quality essential health-care services and

access to safe, effective, quality and affordable essential medicines

and vaccines for all​

Without UHC → fragmented progress and inequity​

Maternal and child health: core outcomes reflecting system strength

Inequalities in health service coverage remain despite global progress​

Maternal & child health outcomes in the UHC framework

Target 3.1: By 2030, reduce the global maternal mortality ratio to less than 70

per 100,000 live births​

Target 3.2: By 2030, end preventable deaths of newborns and children under 5

years of age, with all countries aiming to reduce neonatal mortality to at least

as low as 12 per 1,000 live births and under-5 mortality to at least as low as 25

per 1,000 live births

Linked to UHC monitoring: coverage of maternal and child health services is a key sub-index in the UHC framework.​

Proof of progress: falling mortality indicates better access to life-saving care.​

System quality: high deaths reveal gaps in timely, affordable, quality care​

Interlinked outcomes: maternal and child survival are closely connected — the loss of a

mother greatly increases a child’s risk of dying.

RMNCH outcomes and UHC

RMNCH service is a core component of the UHC SCI.​

Countries that increased overall UHC (e.g., Sierra Leone, Ethiopia, Bangladesh) also show progress in maternal mortality reduction.​

Service coverage helps ensure essential RMNCH care is available and accessible.

Countries that achieved faster reductions in maternal mortality also tended to record

improvements in the RMNCH sub-index.

Conclusion

UHC is critical to sustaining maternal and child health gains and ensuring

equity​

Integrating health service and financing policies is key to expanding both coverage and affordability​

Monitoring SDG 3.1, 3.2, and 3.8 together keeps attention on survival, access, and protection—and accelerates progress to 2030
